Shahriyar Rukun

Computer Science

As a computer science major, the reading Look A White! by George Yancy gave me a unique perspective on the intersection between technology, ethics, race, and systematic biases. Computer science is a field focused on technicality and innovation, but Yancy's readings have expanded my perspective on the broader implications surrounding computer science. One issue I reflected on is the recent surge in the popularity of artificial intelligence and the ethical and moral implications it has on our daily lives. For example, in the reading, Yancy discussed the concept of profiling leading to the mistreatment of minorities due to their racial background. This idea connects to artificial intelligence because humans train AI models, and their functionality depends on the dataset used for training. The dataset's choice has the potential to create AI that can be unethical, immoral, and discriminatory. Unfortunately, creating hateful chatbots is common. One specific example is Microsoft's Tay, a Twitter chatbot released in the mid-2010s trained through conversing with users on social media. Tay was shot down quickly after its release for promoting hate. Tay's example concerns future chatbots and their engagement in social media, news articles, images, and videos. Reflecting on Yancy's discussion on profiling, he discussed that the media's role in reinforcing racial stereotypes and disseminating hateful messages resonates with the potential for artificial intelligence to do the same. The growing usage of AI technologies requires us to be more critical and considerate about its implications for promoting biases. In addition, another area I reflected on in connection to Yancy's readings and my major is the involvement of technology in violence. In the text, he discussed the involvement of government bodies using tactics to harass, profile, and commit violence against black people solely based on their race. Often, software is involved in surveilling individuals, spreading discriminatory practices, and amplifying biases, which can result in systemic injustices within society. This caused me to be conscious of the jobs I apply to, specifically avoiding ones that require me to create software for law enforcement agencies, correctional facilities, or government entities where the technology is utilized to perpetuate discriminatory practices or contribute to systemic injustices against certain racial or ethnic groups. Overall, Look A White! George Yancy complimented my studies in computer science by emphasizing the importance of ethical, racial, and societal considerations in technology. It broadened my perspective on the impacts of technology on society, encouraging me to approach my field with a more ethical mindset.

Psychology

This section of psychology really values ethical practices and focuses a lot on informed consent, especially when conducting research. Something that is used in psychology which relates to “Rap Music”, is that psychology makes very clear distinctions of experience. There are many methods of therapy which make it clear that one’s lived experience can be very separate and different, than another's. This helps with issues relating to cognition and can help individuals deal with past and present issues. Many other methods of psychology which relate to philosophy include behaviorism and humanism which both focus on the behaviors and actions of people as individuals.
